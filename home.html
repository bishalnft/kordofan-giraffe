About Kordofan giraffe

The Kordofan giraffe is a subspecies of giraffe found in northern Cameroon, southern Chad, the Central African Republic, and possibly western Sudan.

Approximately 2000

Habitat:

In addition to their diminishing population size, the Kordofan giraffe inhabits some of the most unstable regions in the world: southern Chad, the Central African Republic, northern Cameroon and northern Democratic Republic of Congo.

Diet:

Completely herbivorous: leaves, grasses, buds, young shoots and seeds

Its spotted coat is similar to other sub-species of giraffes from the north; however it has no markings on its lower legs.

Why are Kordofan giraffe's Important?

Giraffe's are vital to keeping ecosystems in balance. They eat the browse that others cannot reach, which promotes growth of forage and opens up areas for themselves and other smaller browsers to make use of. Importantly this means that by protecting Africa's giraffes, we are protecting other species too.

Why are Kordofan giraffe's Endangered?

All the typical reasons are at play: loss of habitat, civil unrest and poaching. But since giraffes were never afforded protection from trade until 2019 (thanks to the hard work of IFAW and aforementioned conservationists), pointing precisely to illegal trade of their parts is difficult.
